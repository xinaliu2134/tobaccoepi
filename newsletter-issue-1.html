This Week in Tobacco Regulation

FDA CTP Leadership Shakeup

Brian King removed as CTP Director; Bret Koplow named acting director. The leadership change raises questions about pending PMTA reviews and enforcement priorities. Industry watchers expect potential shifts in synthetic nicotine regulation timelines.

Flavor-Specific PMTA Draft Guidance Released

FDA published draft guidance (March 9, 2026) for flavored tobacco product applications, signaling a potential pathway for characterized flavors. This could reshape the PMTA landscape for e-cigarette manufacturers currently limited to tobacco and menthol.

Senate Proposes $200M Enforcement Budget

A bipartisan Senate committee proposed $200M in dedicated funding for illegal vape enforcement, targeting unauthorized imports and retail sales. If approved, this would represent the largest single enforcement allocation in CTP history.

State Vapor Directory Expansion

Pennsylvania becomes the 14th state to adopt a vapor product directory, requiring manufacturers to register before retail sales. This state-level regulatory patchwork continues to increase compliance complexity for national brands.

Market Intelligence

IQOS HeatStick Authorization Expands

Philip Morris International's heated tobacco portfolio continues to gain regulatory traction globally, with new market authorizations strengthening the modified risk narrative. Watch for potential MRTP expansion applications in the US market.

Nicotine Pouch Category Growth

ZYN (Swedish Match / PMI) continues to lead the nicotine pouch segment. EpiDataKit's NYTS 2025 analysis shows emerging youth uptake patterns that merit surveillance — a dataset currently unique to our platform.

From the Lab

E-Cigarette Cardiopulmonary Effects: A Systematic Review

A comprehensive review published in the American Journal of Physiology synthesizes evidence on cardiovascular and pulmonary effects of e-cigarette use, including acute vascular dysfunction, inflammatory biomarker elevation, and long-term respiratory outcomes. Relevant for companies preparing PMTA health impact assessments.

Also notable: Truth Initiative published new data on nicotine dependence trajectories among youth vapers, showing faster onset compared to combustible cigarettes.
