Market Context
  • Overall youth e-cigarette use: 5.2% (down from 5.9% in 2024)
  • Unauthorized brands still dominate — only 2 of top 10 brands (Vuse, JUUL) hold FDA marketing authorization
  • Disposable devices: 66.3% of the market (↑10.7pp year-over-year)
  • FDA enforcement paradox: each banned brand is quickly replaced by a new unauthorized entrant
Implications for Industry
  1. Unauthorized brands: FDA enforcement is inevitable. Geek Bar faces the same fate as Puff Bar and Elf Bar before it.
  2. Authorized brands (Vuse, JUUL): Market share gains possible as enforcement removes competitors, but consumer loyalty to disposable format is the real barrier.
  3. PMTA applicants: Youth brand surveillance data is now a critical input to FDA authorization decisions. Any product in the NYTS top 10 faces heightened scrutiny.
  4. MRTP applicants (ZYN): Youth nicotine pouch use at 1.7% with ZYN at 61.4% usual brand share—lower than e-cigarettes but trending upward.
Methodology

Based on independent weighted analysis of NYTS 2025 public-use file (n=23,630, WT_analysis). All estimates cross-validated against Altria Client Services official analysis (9/9 products match within 0.2pp).
